Hey lill' girl, what's your name?
To identificate our first class, we have to name her. There would be no sense in giving all classes the same name. The JAVA-compiler would have an overflow on senseless errors. Well, our first class will be a girl (I decided that, shut up!). So, we call it "Maedchen"
Fill in the field "Name" the String "Maedchen" and look, we have done the name. A little hint: always choose names that have sense, that makes it easier to work later. If your classes and methods have names like HKlSetKarlMF8 it's hard to orientate in the resulting JAVA-sourcecode. Take names like "vehicle" or "cocktail" or something like that. That's easier.

There are many special features classes can have. Some are including a part called main, others are "abstract" or "final". Some are asserting that they are "interfaces". But at this point that's not the problem. In the progression of this tutorial you are going to learn more 'bout these things.
Back to the girls. We have to declare the group of girls for abstract. Dont't ask me why, but that's the facs. To declare them abstract click "abstract" in the dialog. "Abstract" means, that there will be no further instances of this class and the methods have to be overwritten in all cases. You don't understand this? No problem. You'll learn more 'bout that later.
